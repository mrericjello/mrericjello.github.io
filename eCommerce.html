eCommerce

What is eCommerce?

One of the fundamental areas of Web Development Industry paths is eCommerce. eCommerce has been on the rise since the .com boom of the 1990's. Companies using the "Wizard of Oz" selling technique realized that users were willing to purchase items online such as cars or furniture. Many companies since then have created an eCommerce portion of their stores to sell online items. Places like Ebay, Amazon, and QVC capitalized on this idea as they realized consumers wanted to shop from home. Today many retail stores and restaraunts have a mobile ordering application within their websites to fulfill online orders.

BitCoin

Revisit the Dark Web

Because eCommerce has become an intergral part of internaional economy, many websites have realized a problem. How can you order from an online store if it uses a different currency than you're own? Developers have come up with two solutions: 1. Have a currency converter to tell the user how much a product is in their currency. Users place their order with their currecy, then the company converts the electronic currency with the bank and then fulfill the order after the conversion is made. Or 2. Create a cryptocurrecy that acts as its own currency for electronic transactions.

Henceforth BitCoin was born. BitCoin to many is still a complex issue by economists, financial sectors and the general public. BitCoin has been feared to take over the American Dollar. So what's the big deal about it? Well according to financial and web analysts, BitCoin is a cryptocurrency that carries no real value to it. What is really carries are data filled packets that contain value in currency. This electronic data can be valued by any currency but is ultimately tied back to the American Dollar.

So how do I get my hands on BitCoins? Well the answer is not so simple. BitCoins are mainly in transaction in the Dark Web. Where many illegal activities still occur. But can't the government trace those activities? Actually, because the Dark Web acts through anonymous means of layered encryption, well you guessed it, BitCoins are layered in encryption also. Also, the general ledger system it uses for transactions is so sophisticated that even the financial sector is astounded by it. So much that they want to replicate it's software for world use.
